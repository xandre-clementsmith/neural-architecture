- Orexin-A/hypocretin-1 and Orexin-B/hypocretin-2
- Excitatory neuropeptides
- 50,000-80,000 orexin-producing neurons in the human brain
- Primarily located in the [[Anterior Hypothalamus]] (Perifornical), [[Lateral Hypothalamus]], [[Posterior Hypothalamus]]
- Highly conserved system between rats and humans - peptide is found in all major classes of vertebrates
# Function
- Regulates wakefulness, feeding, energy expenditure, visceral function, thermoregulation, cardiovascular responses, and spontaneous physical activity
- Integrates metabolic, circadian, and sleep debt information to determine whether to be asleep, awake, or active
- Strongly excites [[Dopamine (DA)]], [[Norepinephrine]], [[Histamine]], and [[Acetylcholine (ACh)]] - also regulate [[Serotonin (5-HT)]]
- Increases food craving (may be due to general arousal without necessarily increasing overall food intake)
- May be involved in addiction circuits
